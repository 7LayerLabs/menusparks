**Last Updated:** October 12, 2025
**Status:** Launch Pricing (Founder's Special)

## Executive Summary

MenuSparks pricing is designed for **small independent restaurants** with tight budgets and conservative technology spending. Our research shows typical independent restaurants budget ~$333/month for all software, making affordability critical.

**Key Pricing Principles:**
1. **Accessible Entry Point** - Low weekly prices remove barriers ($3-19/week)
2. **Cash Flow Flexibility** - Weekly OR monthly billing options
3. **Clear Monthly Savings** - Incentivize monthly subscriptions for better retention
4. **Founder's Special Pricing** - Launch with aggressive pricing, increase later
5. **Bundle Opportunities** - Multi-product discounts drive higher LTV

## Pricing Psychology & Strategy

### Why Weekly + Monthly Options?

**Weekly Billing Benefits:**
1. **Lower barrier to entry** - "$12/week" feels more accessible than "$40/month"
2. **Cash flow alignment** - Many small restaurants manage cash week-to-week
3. **Try-before-commit** - Easier to test for a week or two
4. **Psychological framing** - "Cost of two coffees per week"

**Monthly Billing Benefits:**
1. **Better retention** - Monthly subscribers stay longer
2. **Lower transaction costs** - Fewer Stripe fees
3. **Easier budgeting** - One charge per month
4. **Discount incentive** - Saves money vs weekly
5. **Professional positioning** - Industry standard

### Founder's Special Strategy

**Launch Pricing (Now - Month 6):**
- Current aggressive pricing to acquire first 100-500 customers
- Position as "Founder's Special" or "Early Adopter Pricing"
- Lock in customers before price increase

**Standard Pricing (Month 6+):**
- Quick Bites: $15/week or $55/month
- Chef's Choice: $25/week or $95/month
- Industry Newsletter: $5/week or $15/month
- Customized Newsletter: $12/week or $45/month

**Grandfathering:**
- Early adopters keep Founder's Special pricing forever
- Creates loyalty and word-of-mouth marketing
- "Join now before prices go up" urgency

## Payment Processing

### Stripe Integration
- **Transaction Fees:** 2.9% + 30¢ per charge
- **Weekly billing cost:** Higher fees (4 charges/month per customer)
- **Monthly billing cost:** Lower fees (1 charge/month per customer)

**Example Cost Analysis:**
- Weekly $12 charge: $0.65 fee (5.4% effective rate)
- Monthly $40 charge: $1.46 fee (3.65% effective rate)
- **Savings with monthly:** ~1.75% of revenue

**Strategy:** Incentivize monthly with savings while keeping weekly option for flexibility

## Free Trial Strategy

### Option 1: 7-Day Free Trial
- No credit card required
- Access to 1 recipe batch (5 recipes)
- 1 newsletter issue
- Convert to paid after trial

### Option 2: First Week $1
- Credit card required
- Full access to chosen tier
- Auto-renews at regular price
- Lower friction than "free"

### Option 3: No Trial
- Start with lowest tier (Industry Newsletter $10/month)
- Position as "under $3/week, cancel anytime"
- Immediate revenue, lower churn

**Recommendation:** Start with **Option 3** (no free trial), test **Option 2** after first 50 customers.

- Target CAC: $10-50
- Channels: Blog, SEO, social media organic

**Paid Advertising:**
- Target CAC: $50-150
- Channels: Facebook/Instagram ads, Google Ads
- Payback period: 2-3 months

**B2B Outreach (Agent Army):**
- Target CAC: $20-75 per lead
- Perplexity research: $2-5 per lead
- Mockup generation: $5-10 per lead
- Email copywriting: $3-5 per lead
- Total cost per lead: $10-20
- Conversion rate target: 5-10%

**Partnerships:**
- Target CAC: $25-100
- Restaurant associations, supplier partnerships

**Target LTV:CAC Ratio:** 5:1 minimum (Chef's Choice: $735 LTV / $150 CAC = 4.9:1)

## Competitive Response Plan

### If Competitors Lower Prices
1. Emphasize quality and personalization (not a race to bottom)
2. Add value through features, not price cuts
3. Grandfather existing customers
4. Test limited-time promotions

### If Competitors Raise Prices
1. Maintain Founder's Special pricing longer
2. Position as "affordable premium"
3. Capture price-sensitive customers
4. Consider limited price increase (test 10-15%)

### If New Entrants
1. Lock in customers with annual contracts
2. Emphasize track record and testimonials (Bobola's)
3. Build switching costs (recipe library, data history)
4. Add unique features (B2B agent army, market intel)
